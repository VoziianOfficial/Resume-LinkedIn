      Website Data Handling

      This Privacy Policy applies to the NimoMark website, its inquiry form and related website communications.

      NimoMark is an independent career-support information and inquiry platform. Visitors should submit only information reasonably necessary to explain their inquiry.

      This policy does not apply to the separate privacy practices of independent service providers, external websites, email providers or other third parties.

      Effective July 21, 2026 Applies to website inquiries Global audience
      Section 01

      Policy Overview

      NimoMark may collect limited personal information when a visitor submits an inquiry, communicates by email or interacts with essential website functionality.

      Information may be used to receive and review an inquiry, respond to the sender, understand the requested career-support category, maintain website security and, where appropriate, consider whether an independent provider may be relevant.

      Submit only relevant information

      Do not submit passwords, payment-card details, government identification numbers, medical records or unrelated sensitive personal information through the website form.

      Section 02

      Information Collected

      The categories of information collected depend on how a visitor uses the website.

      Category Examples How it may be obtained
      Contact information Full name and email address Submitted directly through the contact form or email
      Inquiry information Current country, inquiry type, selected service, message and relevant career context Submitted directly by the visitor
      Website context Source page, page title and form submission time Added by website functionality when an inquiry is submitted
      Technical information IP address, request headers, browser information and security-related request metadata Processed automatically by website hosting, server and security systems
      Preference information Cookie-consent choice and the date that preference was stored Stored locally in the visitor’s browser

      Visitors are responsible for ensuring that the information they submit is accurate and that they are authorized to provide it.

      Section 03

      Information Use

      Personal information may be used for purposes reasonably connected with website operation and inquiry handling.

      • Receive, review and organize submitted inquiries.
      • Respond to the sender using the provided email address.
      • Understand the requested service category, professional direction or collaboration topic.
      • Consider whether an independent service provider or specialist may be relevant to the inquiry.
      • Prevent spam, automated abuse and repeated excessive submissions.
      • Maintain website functionality, security and technical reliability.
      • Comply with applicable legal obligations and respond to valid legal requests.

      Inquiry information is not intended to be used for unrelated profiling or the sale of personal information to advertisers.

      Section 04

      Legal Permissions

      Depending on the visitor’s location and the circumstances, information may be processed on one or more of the following grounds:

      Consent
      When a visitor voluntarily submits an inquiry and confirms the Privacy Policy checkbox.
      Requested steps
      When processing is reasonably necessary to review or respond to a visitor’s request before any separate service arrangement.
      Legitimate interests
      For operating the website, preventing misuse, protecting systems and managing genuine business communications.
      Legal obligations
      When information must be processed or retained to comply with applicable law or a valid legal request.

      Submitting an inquiry does not create a service contract and does not require the visitor to accept a later proposal.

      Section 05

      Inquiry Handling

      Website inquiries may be delivered to the configured NimoMark business email address and reviewed by authorized persons responsible for inquiry handling.

      1. The visitor submits the contact form and confirms the Privacy Policy checkbox.
      2. The website validates the required fields and applies automated spam and rate-limit checks.
      3. The inquiry is transmitted to the configured recipient email account.
      4. The inquiry may be reviewed to determine whether additional information or a response is appropriate.
      5. Where relevant and permitted, the visitor may be asked whether limited information can be shared with an independent provider.

      Do not send confidential account access

      NimoMark does not need passwords, authentication codes, payment-card details or access to private online accounts in order to review an initial inquiry.

      Section 06

      Service Providers

      NimoMark may rely on third parties that support website operation and communication. These may include:

      • Website-hosting and server providers.
      • Domain, security and infrastructure providers.
      • Business email and message-delivery providers.
      • Technical contractors maintaining website functionality.
      • Independent career-support providers considered relevant to a submitted inquiry.

      Third parties should receive only the information reasonably necessary for their role. Independent providers may have their own privacy policies and act independently when communicating about a possible service.

      NimoMark does not guarantee that a particular independent provider will be available or suitable for every inquiry.

      Section 07

      International Processing

      NimoMark is designed for an international audience. Website hosting, email delivery, technical support or independent provider involvement may result in information being processed in a country different from the visitor’s current country.

      Privacy and data-protection rules may differ between jurisdictions. Where required, reasonable contractual, technical or organizational safeguards may be used for international processing.

      International career support is not legal advice

      Privacy processing associated with an inquiry is separate from immigration, visa, tax, employment-authorization or credential advice.

      Section 08

      Data Retention

      Information may be retained only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, including inquiry review, communication, security, dispute management and applicable legal obligations.

      Inquiry messages
      May remain in business email or related records while the inquiry is active and for a reasonable period afterward.
      Security records
      May be retained temporarily to detect repeated submissions, abuse or technical incidents.
      Cookie preferences
      Remain in the visitor’s browser until removed, replaced or cleared through browser controls.
      Legal records
      May be retained when reasonably necessary to comply with law, resolve disputes or protect legitimate rights.

      Retention periods may vary depending on the communication, the systems involved and applicable requirements.

      Section 09

      Information Security

      Reasonable technical and organizational measures may be used to reduce the risk of unauthorized access, misuse, alteration or disclosure.

      • Form-field validation and request-size limits.
      • Automated honeypot and submission-rate checks.
      • Request-origin and content-type validation.
      • Restricted access to business communications.
      • Security headers and limited browser storage.

      No website, email system or transmission method can be guaranteed to be completely secure. Visitors should avoid submitting unnecessary sensitive information.

      Section 10

      Privacy Choices

      Depending on applicable law and the circumstances, a person may be able to request:

      • Confirmation of whether personal information is being processed.
      • Access to personal information associated with their inquiry.
      • Correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
      • Deletion of information where retention is no longer required.
      • Restriction of or objection to certain processing.
      • Withdrawal of consent where processing depends on consent.
      • Information about an applicable complaint or regulatory process.

      A request may require reasonable verification of identity and enough information to locate the relevant inquiry. Some information may need to be retained when permitted or required by law.

      Browser-stored cookie preferences can be changed through the Cookie Policy or removed using browser controls.

      Section 11

      Children’s Privacy

      NimoMark is intended for adults and professionals seeking career-related information or submitting business and collaboration inquiries.

      The website is not directed to children, and NimoMark does not knowingly seek personal information from children. A parent or guardian who believes that a child submitted personal information may contact NimoMark to request review and appropriate removal.

      Section 12

      Third-Party Links

      The website may contain links to third-party websites, platforms, provider pages or external resources. NimoMark does not control the privacy practices, security or content of those external services.

      Visitors should review the privacy policy and terms of each third-party service before submitting personal information or creating an account.

      A link or reference does not necessarily represent an endorsement, guarantee or continuing commercial relationship.

      Section 13

      Policy Updates

      This Privacy Policy may be updated when website functionality, inquiry handling, service-provider relationships, legal requirements or privacy practices change.

      The effective date displayed on this page indicates when the current version is intended to apply. Material changes may also be communicated through an additional website notice where appropriate.

      Continued use of the website after an update is subject to the revised policy, but any consent required by applicable law will be requested separately.
